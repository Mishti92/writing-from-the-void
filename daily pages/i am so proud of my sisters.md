I love them so much. 

My elder sister just bought a house. she is probably the first woman in our entire extended family to own a property, all on her own. She is the daughter that laid down the path for her younger sisters to follow. The first in her blood to create her own destiny. the first to leave Kashmir, not just to another state but to a new country. the first to challenge the societal norms dictated for women.

For as long as I can remember, I have looked upto her and admired her. I call her *didi*, urdu word for sister. It used to be *badi didi* (the elder sister), until one day I realized that i only have one elder sister and I can cut off the *badi* part.

I have never been much of a reader ( * a habit i am trying to change intentionally*) 

But... 

5th grade me loved to read her short novels. Stories she made up based on the people in her life *( crushes and school friends )*, taking inspiration from her dreams more than once. *something Xanther and a cult.* 

Most of the books I read in my teenage years were the ones she would borrow from the library and bring home. *harry potter, paulo coehlo, sidney sheldon*.

She is the writer in our family. 

My dad also loved to write. He would write love letters to my mom (*i havent verified it from my dad yet, but mom confirms*). And he was the one who got books for my sister to read growing up. She got into reading, and then started writing to express herself. I would tell everyone about her novels and that someday she will be a famous writer, much like John Keats. Writer. Studied Medicine. Minus the *dying early* part.

Her writing did get us into trouble more than once as kids. She would be too honest about her opinions of our relatives in her diary and not too careful with where she would leave her diaries. We got some good quality chappals 🩴🩴 our way that one time. 

my younger sister just got into her dream job. I remember going to Bangalore with her during her college application days to apply to universities for architecture bachelors. She didn't want to be a doctor, she loved to draw, she loved to make dolls out of crochet. She took Medical + Maths in High school - keeping her options open for university. Leaving it on chance or luck or success in entrance exams to either become a doctor or an engineer. Elder sister was studying medicine. The second one, me, was studying computer science engineering (*[[how I got from there to here]]* is another story). She chose architecture. None of the cousins had took that path before. She took that chance and believed that she could do it. And this month, after 5 years of bachelors in Bangalore and 2 years of masters in Torino, Italy, with some internships, projects and what she calls as challenges in there - she's working a job that she absolutely adores. One that lights her up. She feels excited to wake up everyday. She is at peace. And I am so happy for her.
