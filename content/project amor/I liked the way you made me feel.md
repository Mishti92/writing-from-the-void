I might have looked you up yesterday. watched an interview I found on YouTube, glimpsed in a world, a world so familiar to you, yet unknown to me until now.
_It’s strange, isn’t it? How entire worlds exist outside our awareness until someone points them out on the map._

And yet, even without knowing this world, I never felt out of place as you opened the doors for me.

_Maybe it’s the_ _way you invited me in with a smile, a smile so kind and gentle that it could_ soften the sharp edges of a guarded heart

Maybe it’s the way you listened—  
not just to respond, but to understand,  
to meet me where I am.

Maybe it’s the way you made space-  
not just for my words,  
but for my silence, for my breath, for my being.

Maybe that’s why, with you,  
I felt no need to make myself smaller,  
no urge to filter my thoughts or dim my presence.

Maybe that’s why, from the very first moment,  
conversation with you felt effortless
three hours passing like minutes,  
words flowing with no need to fill the silence,  
without the need to prove anything,  
without the need to be anyone but myself.

You met me in the middle.  
Held space for my curiosity, my exploration.  
Your words challenged me,  
but never overpowered.

Your tone, your enthusiasm
they matched my energy, my rhythm, my thoughts,  
in a way that felt like discovery, not competition.

And I liked that.  
I liked the way you made me think.  
I liked the way you made me feel.
