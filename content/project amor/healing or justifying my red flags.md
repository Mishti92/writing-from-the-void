El chico de francés texted me today that he felt it was a bit of red flag – the fact _according to him_ that I didn’t understand that he was busy and I had asked him to apologize.  
_context: I was flirting while being mean!_

As soon as he said that, I felt a bit of an energy shift, something I hadn’t felt in a long time. In healing terms, you would call it “being triggered”. I had jokingly _(no, I was 100% flirting)_ texted him that he should swing by the cafe and apologize as he broke my heart last night.

Yes you may wondering – wtf Shae? You just met this dude a day back. Calm down, will ya?

Okay but maybe I have seen too many bollywood movies where this kind of sarcasm is a very successful way to flirt. But I see your point and I see where it might have gone wrong.

Maybe I am oblivious to another human’s thought process and how my words may be perceived by them. Or maybe it’s the virtual component of it all, where the tone is misinterpreted and a token of love could be misread as an attack, as a cage, as the feeling of salt on the wound, as a reminder of the bad experience from the past. It’s unfortunate but I understand that could happen.

And in response, the self reacts, rather instantly, putting up the walls to protect itself from getting hurt again.

_We are much better than that, we have learned, we have grown, we have healed, we do not self abandon, we do not let anyone hurt us again, we take care of ourselves, we are adults now, who take care of the inner cutie in us. We hug ourselves and we keep ourselves safe._

The walls from the outside look like they are our protectors, making sure the arrows don’t reach the soft corners of the heart. The corners that took forever to mend, remember?  
The corners where the pain still feels fresh. The corners that feel like grief tucked away, that with a gush of wind (aka el triggor ) suddenly flies all over the place. Those corners remember the grief. Those corners remember the pain. The heart remembers the pain and the body remembers the pain.  
And so it reacts, rather instantly.

But wait a second! Was I joking though? Was it a sarcasm? I mean my heart did break a bit, ok?

Maybe I romanticize everything way too soon. Maybe I believe way too much in love and surrender. And in letting go of yourself, a bit, just a bit. Not too much that you will drown but hey, I am not going to lie but it will hurt. You can’t expect to find love without putting away the shields. You can’t avoid being hurt. When you consciously choose to get to know someone, you have to believe in the possibility, you have to believe in magic. You have to surrender and let universe take charge. You have to be a bit delulu, ok? That is just a part of living fully. You have to overcome fear and be slightly crazy to believe in love.  
To believe love is the answer to all the madness.  
And to believe that _‘to love is to live’._
To [[choose love]] over fear.

As my friend Adina reminded me today:  
_the task is not to look for love but to dismantle everything in your heart that you have built up against it! I love you chica!_

So back to the question:  
Am I healing or have I just created a narrative around my old coping patterns? Is it healing or is it me justifying my anxious attachment.

Ahaaan!! But is it anxious attachment? 

So while I did get hurt and yes, that trigger led to a tiny burn on the wounds, that soon turned into a stream of tears. But that had nothing to do with this chico.

I mean, I did feel slightly hurt that he couldn’t meet me. But I also understood that he had to work. And yes, my mind did try to create a narrative that he probably isn’t interested in getting to know me. Or even if he is, he probably isn’t ready to receive love in his life, because other things are taking priority and that’s totally okay.

Even if the narrative was a wall that I have put up to protect myself, a coping pattern from my childhood that self-sabotages at times and now I have intellectualized this “_red flag”._ Even if that was true, I did not react to that narrative. I allowed myself to feel it and in response, my body wanted to cry. And so I did. Maybe if it was any other day, my body would have just reacted by say, huh! and moved on.

So yes, it hurt a bit but why is getting hurt by something you were excited for, considered a red flag!

I don’t get it. I mean I can’t control how something makes me feel but I can control how I react to it. I can control my actions. I can be a mature adult and hold space for my hurt, without acting on it. Why would that be a red flag? That’s a very human thing to feel. Why have we demonized emotions? Why do we feel we need to numb down our feelings so we don’t come across as red flags? Should I be pretending that I don’t have feelings? That I a chill chica and I am cool! And I am totally okay with modern casual dating and whatever these young people call situationships these days. No, I am not okay with that. I will not let anyone ruin love for me. I love _love_ and I will protect the part of me that believes in love.

But….

Maybe I should slow down sometimes.  
I knowwwww.  
_Tranquila Shae!_

But I can’t help how I am, you know! This is how I function. Let me try to reintroduce myself:

_Hi, I am a chica that believes that the purpose of my life is to love._

And for some reason I thought I should hide that information but I find it bizarre that we are all looking for love but we do not want to admit it. And yes, I know all about self love and filling yourself with love before you seek it in others. And that you should not go grocery shopping when you are hungry. And believe me, I have immense self love. I can write an A+ book on self love. I had to build that self love algorithm from scratch. I know that because I wouldn’t be alive today if I didn’t seek that self love at some point.

But self love can co-exist with romantic love. Write that on my grave!

We are not meant to do it alone. We need people around us that care for us, that help us grow, that push us, that lift us up. These people become our community, whether friends, family or friendly faces from the street.

And I don’t know about you but somedays I crave romantic love, even with all the self love. Even with all the community love. Somedays I crave romantic love.  
I crave how it makes me feel, how it brings magic to the mundane, how a lover’s smile can brighten my day, how looking towards him would make my cheeks red, how his presence would make me giggle, how his laugh would make me twirl and how his touch would make me dance. I crave that kind of love.

It’s not the dinner you eat to satisfy your hunger. You are not hungry. You know that. You have a craving and it feels the best after dinner. It’s the midnight craving that makes you grab ice cream from the fridge because you just _have to have it_.  
That is what romantic love feels to me. And this is what I want. And I know, deep in our hearts, we all want that.

Everyone wants to find love but people don’t understand that you don’t just find love. You choose love. You grow love. You sustain love. You water love. You coddle love. You nourish love. You feed love. And it doesn’t just happen. You have to consciously choose it, again and again, and again, until that choice doesn’t make any sense.

_You don’t just fall in and out of love. You choose to fall in and out of love._
