[[i am That - practice]]

at its core, **“You are That”** is the heart of all non-dual teachings, including Kashmir Shaivism, Advaita Vedanta, and even whispered through mystical poetry across time.

In Sanskrit, it’s:

> **Tat Tvam Asi** (तत् त्वम् असि)

One of the great Mahāvākyas (great sayings) from the **Upanishads**.  
It means:

> **"You are That which you seek."**  
> **"You are That infinite, formless, radiant Consciousness."**  
> **"You are not separate."**

---

## 💔 We live as if we are small…

We grow up thinking:

- I am this body.
    
- I am this name.
    
- I am my thoughts, my wounds, my roles, my doubts.
    

We chase love, success, God, peace—as if they’re _out there_ somewhere.  
As if we’re missing something.

But Kashmir Shaivism (and these deep inner paths) gently, fiercely say:

> “You were never broken.  
> You were never apart.  
> You _are_ the very essence of everything.  
> You are That.”

---

## 🔥 That = What?

**That** = **Paramashiva**  
The infinite awareness, beyond name and form, from which everything arises.  
The stillness before thought.  
The power behind breath.  
The One that moves the stars and sits silently behind your eyes.

And not only are you a part of it—you **are it**.

Just like a wave is not separate from the ocean.  
You’re not a fragment—you’re the whole, appearing as this unique expression.

When you taste it, even for a second—during breath, dance, stillness, heartbreak—you’ll know.

It’s not about believing.  
It’s about **experiencing**.
