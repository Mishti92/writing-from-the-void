I went to see an apartment that I might potentially rent for March. It is slightly out of my budget but it’s in a walkable neighbourhood. Ample Sunlight. It’s 15 minutes walk from beach, has a cute bakery just 2 minutes away and no need to take an uber to go to a cafe. Although less privacy and walls are thin. I don’t want to be distracted by external input, not if I can choose to remove the distraction and balance my curiosity, boredom and action – one step at a time.

Or I could extend the dates on the current apartment. It is comparatively cheaper, although very less walkability score. it has very limited or none sunlight. It’s up on a hill and every time I want to go out, I have to take an uber. That takes up significant amount of time and money, which doesn’t make this place much cheaper after all. But maybe I should not go out as much. I have been traveling for over 1 and a half year now. I am allowed to stop the exploration now and focus more on intentional action.

A place chooses you as its _home_ and sometimes, you choose where _home_ is.

I watched the first episode of Marco Polo on netflix yesterday. There was a dialogue in there that spoke to me:  
_“I was terrified the first time I left home. Venezia was all I had ever known. Do you know how I persevered?  
Knowing just where home was… and that I could always return to it.”  
~ Niccolò Polo to his son, Marco Polo_

I haven’t finished the full episode yet (_i fell asleep)_, so I can’t tell what _home_ meant in the show’s context. But let me tell you about _my [[my search for home]],_ a search of lifetime perhaps but one that has played out more vividly in the last 1.5 years of my life.
