  
For the past 1.5 year, I have been on the road, traveling from city to city, country to country, and as of today, finding myself in this beautiful country of Brazil. My travels started as a search for _home,_ while being away from everything I had previously called _home._ 

I did not set out to travel the world, to be _homeless,_ to wander around aimlessly. It was not planned. 

It was, however, an act of surrender to the workings of the universe. 

_To let myself flow with the water._ 

To let the waves take me wherever the wind blows. 

To surrender to the way of the universe. 

I did not choose to surrender but the universe gave me no other option but to let go. As they say, I was on my knees andI no longer had to courage to hold onto anything. 

_I had to let myself flow with the water._

For someone who has been a control freak all her life, _this was a huge change._

I once took my sister to Istanbul, for her birthday trip and the control freak in me, aka la perfeccionista wanted it to be perfecto. I had downloaded this app that helps you plan your trip [ a un punto ]. And for the three days of that trip, I filled it with a detailed itinerary of what we would be doing, where we would be staying, where we would be eating, where we would enjoy our evenings and where we would go for a day visit.  I had booked this amazing airbnb in Beyoglu, next to Galata Tower. That first day, we met at the Istanbul Airport as she was arriving from Italy and I was coming from UK, after being with my elder sister for a while. That month, in the February of 2022, was one of the first times I had gone for my solo travels, the purpose being to spend some time with my sisters. 

And just an hour in, after arriving to our airbnb, I had a major outburst. Our airbnb didn’t turn out as I had imagined. It wasn’t cleaned and it was almost nothing like the pictures. I immediately went into a fix mode, contacting the airbnb owner to cancel it but also panicking that we don’t have a place to stay now and the whole birthday trip is going to get ruined. I didn’t speak Turkish and for the first time, I am navigating a country where we don’t share a common language. 

We found a hotel eventually but the whole situation had shook me so badly that my sister had to calm me down and reassure me that we will still have a good time. And that she doesn’t care about anything but to spend time with me. We did end up having an amazing time for those 3 days. Minus the end when I got food poisoning and my boyfriend at the time had to come all the way to Istanbul to take me to the hospital. 

Fun times.  

_side note:_

_If you have a sister, you should definitely travel with her as an adult. It’s the most precious relationship I have experienced in this life. I feel so connected to my sisters. I feel so much love for them. Sometimes, it feels like we are all one soul, living in different bodies and experiencing life a bit differently. I have also come to experience the same sisterhood with my chicas I have met on these travels and those are some of the best memories I have of the past 1.5 years._

A few months after this birthday trip, I saw a post on Instagram that said something along the lines of _“the parts of you that want everything to be in control, are the parts that need the most healing”._

The universe knew what it was doing. And so, October 2023, I started my solo travels, stepping in the unknown to finally get to know myself and surrendering to the plans of the universe.

It was a big thing for me - someone who 100% believed that she can’t let anything slip. Everything has to be perfect. Everything has to be in control. Everyone has to be happy with her. That her worthiness was tied to what she could do for others, and how happy, pleased and satisfied they were with her. That she can’t let anyone see the mess and chaos within her. No one can see her as a human - a human trying to figure out who she is, just like anyone else. A human who doesn’t have all the answers, who makes mistakes, who feels lost, who craves love but didn’t know how to give it to herself. 

I did not know surrender. But I knew self-abandonment.
I knew how to let myself go, to slip away from my own being and pour everything I had into someone else. I knew how to attach deeply, how to wrap my identity around another person, so tightly that I could no longer tell where they ended and where I began. I knew how to make a home in someone else, how to dissolve into their presence, their words, their love—until I forgot how to exist on my own.

I knew how to lose myself, so I would find myself in others. 

And we all know how that movie ends. 

On what happens when this home is taken away from you and now you are homeless. You feel empty, you feel lost, you feel disconnected from everything you had previously associated with. 

I used to say I had abandonment issues. Turns out I had issues of abandoning myself again and again, in the hope that other’s won’t abandon me. 

I had it all wrong. I was making a home in people, places and things -  all external to me. Home that could easily be taken away from me. That’s not the home that’s going to be a safe space for my body. That can’t be the base. 

We need a firm ground to stand on, one that can handle the chaos of the world. A home that is resilient enough to stand strong when there’s a huge storm. A home that can handle the changing seasons, because if there’s one thing that’s certain - it’s that we will change as time changes.  A home that can expand and make space for new beginnings and let go of the old. A home that does not cling to fear but trusts in the natural cycles of life—birth, growth, death, and rebirth. A home that does not crumble when the past knocks on the door or when uncertainty lingers in the air.

A home that holds you in your fullness. In your joy, in your sorrow. In your loudest laughter and your quietest tears. 

A home that whispers, _You are safe here._ _You belong here._ _You are enough, exactly as you are._

_easy peasy, shae!_ 

I know, easier said than done.

To begin with, not only I never felt at home in my body, in my soul, in my heart, I also didn’t know how to make myself feel safe and secure. How to be my own safe space. How to give love to myself. 

_I knew self-love_ , but I didn’t know what it meant. 

It seemed like something only the few have access to, and the rest of us are just meant to admire and talk about _this_ magical potion from far and beyond.

I think most of us know how to love others, how to cater to their needs, and how to show up for them. But we don’t know how to love ourselves or even how we want to be loved by ourselves. 

How to listen to our own needs and take action and responsibility by showing up for ourselves? How to hug ourselves when we feel heavy emotions—emotions that overwhelm us and make us feel numb at times? How to create space for those emotions and hold ourselves as we breathe and just exist? 

To hold space for our joy, our excitement, our sorrows, our hurt, our grief, and most of all, our love. How to soothe, please, and tease ourselves. How to speak kindly to ourselves. How to feed ourselves with love, care and deep respect for our bodies. 

How to stop inflicting harm on our bodies. How to respect and honor ourselves. 

How to show up for our dreams, ambitions and desires that feel true to us, that make us feel alive. 

How to fill the void in ourselves, that we have tried to fill previously, _rather unsuccessfully,_ with people, places and things. And so my travels started as a search for _home,_ this time, within myself. 

I knew I couldn’t outsource self-love anymore, and that I had to figure out what it meant for me. I had to get to know myself, outside of everything I had previously associated to. I had to know myself deeply, so I could surrender and let go, without losing myself. 

it is necessary ton know yourself deeply to surrender to someone without getting lost from yourself
